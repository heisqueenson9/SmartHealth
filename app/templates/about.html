About the Engine

Smart Health Sync
Framework

A multi-class clinical decision-support system built as a final-year Computer Science project at the University of Ghana. It uses four supervised learning algorithms to predict six disease categories from 24 normalized biomarkers — achieving 95.1% accuracy with Random Forest as the production classifier.

Core Mandate

Clinical Decisions, Elevated by AI

The primary objective of Smart Health Sync is to develop a reliable clinical screening tool that utilizes supervised learning models to predict disease states from patient blood panel data. By ensembling multiple classification architectures, the project achieves standardized diagnostics for chronic and hematological conditions.
