The SmartHealth AI Project

A technical overview of the development, architecture, and clinical orientation of SmartHealth AI. This final year project bridges the gap between diagnostic biomarkers and computational inference.

Core Mandate

The primary objective of SmartHealth AI is to develop a reliable clinical screening tool that utilizes supervised learning models to predict disease states from patient blood panel data. By ensembling multiple classification architectures, the project achieves standardized diagnostics for chronic and hematological conditions.

System Architecture

01

Data Preprocessing

Raw clinical biomarkers are normalized onto a standard distribution using scikit-learn's preprocessing pipelines to ensure feature scaling consistency.

02

Classification Model

The optimized Random Forest engine performs multi-class inference across 24 clinical features to identify optimal decision boundaries.

03

Clinical Validation

Diagnostic probability scores are weighted by model confidence metrics to provide a realistic assessment for medical review.
